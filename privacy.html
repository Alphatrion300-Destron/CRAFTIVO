Research & Academic Policy

This page outlines the research ethics, data handling principles, and academic standards followed in projects developed by Collins Egerton, an MIT Computer Science & Artificial Intelligence student.

01

Introduction

This portfolio represents academic and research work conducted at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. All projects adhere to strict engineering, scientific, and ethical standards.

The goal is to develop intelligent systems that are transparent, reproducible, and beneficial to real-world applications in AI, robotics, and data science.

02

Research Data Use

All datasets used in projects are either publicly available, ethically sourced, or synthetically generated for simulation purposes.

  • Machine learning training datasets are anonymized
  • Personal data is never collected or stored
  • Simulation environments are used for robotics testing
  • All research follows MIT academic integrity guidelines
03

Data Collection in Projects

Simulation Data

Used for training autonomous systems in controlled environments such as robotics simulations and AI testing frameworks.

Sensor Data

In robotics projects, data is collected from cameras, LiDAR, and inertial sensors for real-time decision making.

04

Research Ethics

All research follows ethical AI principles including fairness, transparency, accountability, and safety in autonomous systems.

No harmful or biased datasets are used
AI models are tested for fairness and accuracy
All experiments are reproducible
05

Data Security

Secure Storage

Research data stored securely using encrypted cloud systems.

Access Control

Only authorized research environments can access datasets.

Model Integrity

AI models are validated to ensure safe deployment in simulations.

06

Publication Policy

Research findings may be published in academic journals, conferences, or MIT internal research repositories.

All publications follow peer-review standards and include proper citations of datasets, algorithms, and methodologies.

07

Academic Integrity

All work is original unless otherwise cited. Collaboration is acknowledged appropriately in all research outputs.

  • No plagiarism in code or research writing
  • Proper attribution of all sources
  • Ethical collaboration in team projects
